Accommodation for SBT Volunteers in Delhi NCR

Accommodation for SBT Volunteers

MSF Educational Trust facilitates safe, affordable, and comfortable accommodation for SBT (Short-Based Training) Volunteers working across Delhi NCR.

We understand that a secure living environment is essential for volunteers to focus fully on their service and learning experience. Our accommodation arrangements are designed to ensure comfort, safety, and accessibility.

Safe & Supportive Living Spaces

Accommodation for SBT volunteers is arranged in trusted residential facilities located near programme centres and public transport routes within Delhi NCR.

These spaces promote a community living experience where volunteers from diverse backgrounds can interact, share experiences, and support one another.

Facilities Provided

Secure Stay

Safe residential environments with basic security measures and responsible supervision.

Clean & Comfortable Rooms

Shared or individual accommodation options with clean sleeping arrangements and basic furniture.

Essential Amenities

Access to electricity, water supply, washrooms, and common living areas.

Connectivity

Locations with easy access to public transport and nearby essential services.

Eligibility & Guidelines

Accommodation support is available for SBT volunteers who are engaged in full-time volunteering assignments with MSF Educational Trust in Delhi NCR.

Volunteers are expected to follow basic house rules, respect shared spaces, and maintain discipline to ensure a harmonious living environment for all residents.

Location Coverage

Accommodation facilities are currently available across key locations in Delhi NCR, depending on programme needs and volunteer deployment.

  • Central & South Delhi
  • North & West Delhi
  • Noida & Greater Noida
  • Gurugram
  • Ghaziabad

Accommodation Support & Assistance

Our coordination team assists volunteers with accommodation details during onboarding. For specific queries related to availability, facilities, or duration of stay, volunteers are encouraged to connect with the programme coordinator.
